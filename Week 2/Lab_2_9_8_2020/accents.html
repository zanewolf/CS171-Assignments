How does a New Yorker accent and a Bostonian accent differ?

The Bostonian accent is best categorized as a non-rhotic accent. That is - you don't pronounce the 'r' sounds. Image how a pirate growls. 'Arrrgghh!', right? Now, instead, say "Ahhhh" but say it like you're angry, and you've just become a non-rhotic pirate. Most spoken languages are non-rhotic, in fact, or at least mostly non-rhotic. A lot of languages keep the inner r's - those buried inside the words, like "bureaucracy" (which you'll find a lot of at HARVARD, but not the ones at the ends of words - 'inna' vs 'inner'.
